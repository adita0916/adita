Toronto Traffic Collisions

Objectives

The goals of this project are:
-Inspect the dataset
-Based on the inspection, come up with questions that can be answered with the dataset/div>
-Extract information from the dataset with these questions in mind

Tools

-Python
-Numpy
-Pandas
-Plotly

Questions

1. Number of collisions per year
2. Number of collision per month per year
3. Number of collisions per hours
4. Number of collisions per month per division
5. Number of collisions per collision type per year

Data

The dataset was taken from Kaggle. There are 499,539 data with 19 columns. It covers from 2014 to June 2021. https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/andriyahapov/traffic-collisions-toronto

Dashboard and Key Insights

The number of accidents is gradually increasing from 2014 to 2019. 2019 has the most number of accident at 82.8K. It then plummeted in 2020.

December and January are the months with most number of accidents. In 2014 and 2019, the most accidents occurred in January. December had the most accidents in 2016 and 2017.

The most likely hour for traffic accidents are between 15:00 â€“ 17:00.

Regardless of the year, Division 32 and Division 42 came close to the most number of accidents per year. Non-specified Area(NSA) is exempted from ranking as it is combined number outside of the listed divisions.

Collisions were categorized into injury, FTR(Fail To Remain) and PD(Property Damage). Property damages occurred overwhelmingly commonly than the other two types combined.
